Mixtures of Probabilistic Principal Component Analysis (MPPCA)

The model can be formulate as follow:

\[\underline{x_i} = \mathbf{W_g}\underline{u_{ig}} + \underline{\mu_g} + \underline{\epsilon_{ig}}\]

 where 

  • \(\underline{x_i}\) is the vector of observed variables for subject i.
  • \(\mathbf{W_g}\) is the \(p \times q\) loadings matrix within group g
  • \(\underline{u_{ig}}\) is the vector of Gaussian latent variable or it can be viewed as principal score of subject i within group g
  • \(\underline{\mu_g}\) is the mean vector of subject i within group g
  • \(\underline{\epsilon_{ig}}\) is the multivariate Gaussian noise for observation i within group g

The model has a few assumptions in order to obtain the parameter estimates:

  1. \(\underline{\epsilon_{ig}} \sim MVN(\underline{0}, \sigma^2 \mathbf{I})\)
  2. \(\underline{u_{ig}} \sim MVN(\underline{0}, \mathbf{I})\) 

where \(\mathbf{I}\) is the Identity matrix

Model Algorithm:

  1. Initialise loadings matrix, \(\mathbf{W}\), and sigma parameter, \(\sigma\) 
  2. Calculate the conditional expectation of the score, \(\mathbb{E}(\underline{u_i}|...)\) (E step) 
  3. Calculate the maximum likelihood function with respect to the parameters and re-initialize them (M step)
  4. Repeat step 2 and 3 until convergence criterion is achieved or until maximum iteration is arrived.
