Probabilistic Principal Component Analysis and Covariates (PPCCA)

The model can be formulate as follow:

\[\underline{x_i} = \mathbf{W}\underline{u_i} + \underline{\mu} + \underline{\epsilon_i}\]

 where 

  • \(\underline{x_i}\) is the vector of observed variables for subject i
  • \(\mathbf{W}\) is the \(p \times q\) loadings matrix
  • \(\underline{u_i}\) is the vector of Gaussian latent variable or it can be viewed as principal score of subject i
  • \(\underline{\mu}\) is the mean vector of subject i
  • \(\underline{\epsilon_i}\) is the multivariate Gaussian noise for observation i

The model has a few assumptions in order to obtain the parameter estimates:

  1. \(\underline{\epsilon_i} \sim MVN(\underline{0}, \sigma^2 \mathbf{I})\)
  2. For PPCA: \(\underline{u_i} \sim MVN(\underline{0}, \mathbf{I})\)     while     For PPCCA: \(\underline{u_i} \sim MVN(\) \(\underline{\delta_i}\) \(, \mathbf{I})\)  which \(\underline{\delta_i}=\alpha\underline{C_i}\)  

where,

\(\mathbf{I}\) is the Identity matrix

\(\underline{\delta_i}\) is the expected value of the score of observation \(i\) 

\(\alpha\) is the parameter matrix indicating the covariates influence

\(\underline{C_i}\) is the actual covariates value taken from the covariates data

Model Algorithm:

  1. Initialise loadings matrix, \(\mathbf{W}\), and sigma parameter, \(\sigma\) (also \(\underline{\delta_i}\), \(\alpha\) if for PPCCA)
  2. Calculate the conditional expectation of the score, \(\mathbb{E}(\underline{u_i}|...)\) (E step) 
  3. Calculate the maximum likelihood function with respect to the parameters and re-initialize them (M step)
  4. Repeat step 2 and 3 until convergence criterion is achieved or until maximum iteration is arrived.
