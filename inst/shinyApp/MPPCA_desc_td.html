Mixtures of Probabilistic Principal Component Analysis (MPPCA)

The model can be formulate as follow:

\[\underline{x_i} = \mathbf{W_g}\underline{u_{ig}} + \underline{\mu_g} + \underline{\epsilon_{ig}}\]

 where 

  • \(\underline{x_i}\) is the vector of observed variables for subject i.
  • \(\mathbf{W_g}\) is the \(p \times q\) loadings matrix within group g
  • \(\underline{u_{ig}}\) is the vector of Gaussian latent variable or it can be viewed as principal score of subject i within group g
  • \(\underline{\mu_g}\) is the mean vector of subject i within group g
  • \(\underline{\epsilon_{ig}}\) is the multivariate Gaussian noise for observation i within group g

The model has a few assumptions in order to obtain the parameter estimates:

  1. \(\underline{\epsilon_{ig}} \sim MVN(\underline{0}, \sigma^2 \mathbf{I})\),     note: \(\sigma_g = \sigma\) (i.e. equal error variance across all groups)
  2. \(\underline{u_{ig}} \sim MVN(\underline{0}, \mathbf{I})\) 
  3. \(p(\underline{x_i}) = \sum_{g=1}^{G} \pi_g p(\underline{x_i}|\underline{\mu_g},\Sigma_g)\) (i.e. observation is drawn from finite mixture distribution with G components)

where,

  • \(\mathbf{I}\) is the Identity matrix
  • \(\pi_g\) is the mixing proportion which denotes the probability of an observation belonging to group g. \(( 0 \leq \pi_g \leq 1 , \quad \sum_{g=1}^{G} \pi_g = 1 )\)

Model Algorithm:

  1. Initialise cluster groups \(\pi_g\) on the observed data.
  2. Initialise loadings matrix, \(\mathbf{W_g}\), and sigma parameter, \(\sigma\) within respective groups. 
  3. Calculate the expected group membership, \(\tau_{ig}\), of each observation. (E step 1st cycle)
  4. Calculate the maximum likelihood function with respect to \(\underline{\mu_g}\) and \(\pi_g\). (M step 1st cycle)
  5. Calculate the conditional expectation of the score, \(\mathbb{E}(\underline{u_{ig}}|...)\) and recalculate \(\tau_{ig}\). (E step 2nd cycle) 
  6. Calculate the maximum likelihood function with respect to \(\mathbf{W_g}\) and \(\sigma\). (M step 2nd cycle)
  7. Then, re-initialize these parameters and repeat step 3 to 6 until convergence criterion is achieved.
